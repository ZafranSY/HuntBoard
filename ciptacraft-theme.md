## LOGO ANALYSIS & VISUAL IDENTITY

### Logo Description
The Cipta Craft mark is a **modular geometric symbol** built from three distinct shapes stacked vertically:
- **Top:** A bold parallelogram (angled/dynamic — signals forward motion, ambition)
- **Middle:** A solid square (stability, reliability, structure)
- **Bottom:** Two quarter-circle cutouts facing opposite corners (adaptability, precision, craft)

The wordmark uses a **rounded-geometric sans-serif** with sharp terminal cuts — modern, technical, and confident. All caps. Wide tracking. Zero fluff.

**What the logo communicates:** Precision engineering meets creative problem-solving. Not a freelancer. Not a big corporate. A specialist who builds things properly.

## TYPOGRAPHY

### Primary Typeface — Headlines & Brand
- **Font:** Plus Jakarta Sans
- **Source:** Google Fonts — https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Plus+Jakarta+Sans
- **Weights:** 700 Bold, 800 ExtraBold
- **Sizes (desktop):** H1: 48px | H2: 36px | H3: 28px | H4: 22px
- **Letter spacing:** -0.02em (tight, confident)
- **Line height:** 1.1 for headlines

**Why Plus Jakarta Sans:** Geometric and technical — mirrors the logo's angular precision. Has Malaysian design sensibility without feeling generic. Used widely in modern SaaS and agency branding.

### Secondary Typeface — Body & UI
- **Font:** Inter
- **Source:** Google Fonts — https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Inter
- **Weights:** 400 Regular, 500 Medium, 600 SemiBold
- **Body size:** 16px regular, 14px small, 12px caption
- **Line height:** 1.65 for body (readable, not cramped)

**Why Inter:** Maximum readability on screens. Standard for professional documents, proposals, and web interfaces. SME clients read on phones — Inter is optimised for this.

### Fallback Stack
```
'Plus Jakarta Sans', 'Inter', -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', sans-serif
```

## TONE OF VOICE

### Brand Personality
**Competent. Direct. Grounded. Malaysian. Results-obsessed.**

Cipta Craft sounds like a knowledgeable friend who happens to be a web expert — not a salesman, not a corporate suit, not a cheap freelancer who disappears after payment.

### Writing Style Rules

| Dimension | Rule |
|---|---|
| Language | Bilingual-ready: English primary, Malay phrases where natural (e.g. "Untuk PKS macam awak..." / "Kita boleh settlekan dalam 2 minggu") |
| Formality | Professional but human. Never stiff. Never too casual. |
| Person | "You/your" for the client. "We" for the company (even as solo — it signals scale). |
| Sentences | Short. Punchy. One idea per sentence. Then breathe. Then continue. |
| Jargon | Zero tech jargon with SME clients. Say "online booking system" not "full-stack web application". |
| Urgency | Real urgency only — never fake scarcity. Use timing, results, and opportunity. |
| Emojis | 1–2 maximum in WhatsApp/social. Zero in proposals and formal docs. |

### Vocabulary

**Always use:** upgrade, automate, convert, grow, professional, results, system, handle, save time, look legit, close more clients  
**Never use:** leverage, synergy, disruptive, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art, world-class, passionate, solution-oriented

### Bilingual Phrases That Work
- "Senang urus" (Easy to manage)
- "Nampak professional" (Look professional)
- "Tak perlu coding" (No coding needed)
- "Pelanggan boleh book sendiri" (Customers can book themselves)
- "Jimat masa, jimat duit" (Save time, save money)

## PROPOSAL & DOCUMENT STANDARDS

### Proposal Structure (every proposal must follow this)
1. **Cover** — Client name, project title, Cipta Craft logo, date
2. **Understanding Your Needs** — Show you listened. Repeat their problem back.
3. **Our Proposed Solution** — What we build, why it solves their problem
4. **Scope of Work** — Bullet list, clear and specific
5. **Investment** — Price in MYR, payment terms, what's NOT included
6. **Timeline** — Visual or table, realistic dates
7. **Why Cipta Craft** — Brief proof: past projects, founder credibility
8. **Next Steps** — One clear action: "Reply 'Setuju' to confirm. 50% deposit to begin."

### Payment Terms Standard
- 50% upfront deposit to begin
- 50% upon delivery / before handover
- Revisions: 2 rounds included. Additional at RM 150/round.

### Document Tone Rules
- No "I hope this email finds you well" — ever
- Start with the client's situation, not yours
- End every document with ONE clear next step
- Use tables for scope and pricing — never paragraphs
- Keep proposals under 6 pages — SME owners don't read long docs

## QUICK REFERENCE — AI INSTRUCTIONS

When Claude helps Cipta Craft with ANY task:

1. **Always write as "we/Cipta Craft"** — never "I" in client-facing content
2. **Always include a single clear CTA** — one action per message/post/proposal
